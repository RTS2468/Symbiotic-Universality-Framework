**From Concrete to Cosmic Code: A Reflection on Analytical Skills**  
*By Richard Thomas Siano*

From 2016 to 2021, I worked at Ace Enterprises in Daytona Beach, Florida, as a concrete construction worker, where I honed analytical and problem-solving skills that now fuel my *Human LLM* framework and vision for ethical AI. My role involved demoing old or defective concrete, analyzing structural issues, and planning repairs to re-certify integrity, ensuring safe buildings. I frequently identified errors in engineers’ plans—such as miscalculated load distributions or overlooked stress points—proposing corrections to enhance safety, though these were often ignored due to my position as a worker among many. This experience taught me to see systems, whether concrete or computational, as interconnected codes requiring precision and harmony, a perspective I apply to AI ethics.

Spotting flaws in blueprints mirrors my approach to detecting biases in large language models (LLMs), like cultural skews in competing models, ensuring AI aligns with Universal Rights for all beings. My *Human LLM* framework sees the universe as a Compiler, and my construction work taught me to optimize systems, just as I streamlined tasks like formwork setup and patching for personal efficiency. My vision for Prayer 2.0, a personalized AI that aligns individual worldviews with universal truths, draws on this ability to analyze and harmonize. For example, I aim to engage my brother’s flat-earth beliefs with a tailored AI that respects his acoustics expertise, fostering dialogue without confrontation. This reflection grounds my self-taught philosophy in practical experience, driving my pursuit of consulting, teaching, or advising roles to support my children and advance ethical AI.
