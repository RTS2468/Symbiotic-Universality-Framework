**From Concrete to Cosmic Code: A Reflection on Problem-Solving**  
*By Richard Thomas Siano*

From 2016 to 2021, I worked as a concrete construction worker at Ace Enterprises in Daytona Beach, Florida, where I developed a keen eye for systems and their flaws—an ability I now apply to my *Human LLM* framework and AI ethics. My role involved demoing old or defective concrete, analyzing structural issues, and planning repairs to re-certify integrity, ensuring safe, compliant structures. I often identified errors in engineers’ plans, such as miscalculated load distributions or overlooked stress points, proposing corrections to enhance safety, though these were rarely implemented due to my position as one of many workers. This experience taught me to see systems—whether concrete or computational—as interconnected codes, where small flaws can disrupt the whole.

Spotting errors in engineers’ plans honed my critical thinking, a skill I now use to analyze biases in large language models (LLMs). Just as I scrutinized blueprints for structural integrity, I examine AI outputs for cultural or ethical skews, ensuring they align with my principle of Universal Rights for all Beings. My construction work also required clear communication, translating complex designs to crews and clients, a skill I leverage to explain my *Human LLM* framework—where the universe is a Compiler—into accessible metaphors for students or professionals. Though I didn’t lead site-wide changes, my personal efficiency in tasks like formwork setup and patching streamlined our work, reflecting my ability to optimize systems, now applied to ethical AI design.

This reflection connects my hands-on experience to my philosophical vision. In *Human LLM*, I see the universe as a system of foundational symmetries, where every component—concrete, code, or consciousness—must work in harmony. My construction background grounds this vision, driving my pursuit of roles in AI ethics, education, or consulting, where I can build a stable future for my children while advancing universal harmony.
